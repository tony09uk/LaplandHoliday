What to wear in Lapland

Packing for your Lapland holiday can open up lots of questions, like what materials are best, how much should I pack, can I get away with budget brands? Below I have created a list of things to help you decide what to take with you on your dream trip to Lapland.
As always please get in touch if you have any questions, comments or suggestions and checkout the our partners.

What to Wear on a Holiday to Rovaniemi

Winter Attire

LAYERS, LAYERS, LAYERS :) By wearing several thin layers (NOT COTTON - it doesn't trap heat), you'll stay warmer than just one thick layer, the trapped warm air between the layers serves as insulation. If you remove a layer, the reduced trapped heat will cause you to cool down. During the winter months, Rovaniemi can get very cold, with temperatures dropping as low as -40°C. While we were there it was a low of -22°C and a high of -3°C.

Price of clothes

The cost of clothes for such low temperatues can be quite steep and I was advised to buy the best I could afford to. The reality was I couldn't really afford the to kit our family of 4 out in all the top branded winter clothing.
Remember the advice about layers (and cotton) and you may find that you can simply raid your wardrobe, with the need for only a few small aditions. Anything wool, polyester, nylon, silk or down will be a great addition to your suitcase. This can include fleecy tracksuits, pajamas or hoodies.
A good base layer is a a worth while investment, but not one I personally made as I found some old Primark thermal vasts and Long John's in my drawers - they kept me toasty warm :)
The only thin I felt like I needed to buy was a coat and a pair of snow boots. The coat was a goose down O'Neil ski jacket which I picked up from Ebay for £30 and the boots were bought in a sale at Mountain warehouse
The take away from this should be that you probably have most of what you need already and anything you are short of could be bought from the likes of Primark or Decathalon or even E-Bay (if you want to grab a top brand on a budget). The key is, being organised and planning ahead. Get your clothes out early and identify what you need, then you can keep an eye out on the sales and a watch on sites like EBay and Vinted.

What should I pack

Head
  • Wooly Hat
  • Balaclava
  • Snood

Body
  • Thermal base layer
  • Long sleeved vest
  • Fleecy top / Hoodie / Wooly jumper
  • Winter coat / Ski jacket
  • Glove liners / normal wollen gloves
  • Mittens (to be the out layer for the above)
  • Hand warmers

Legs
  • Thermal base layer / Long Johns
  • Fleecy jogging bottoms / Fleecy PJ bottoms
  • Salopettes

Feet
  • Thin socks
  • Thick socks
  • Snow boots

Conclusion

The above should serve as a guide and help you identify what you are missing from your wardrobe.
Remember having layers gives you flexibilty and will allow you to enjoy you dream holiday in Lapland. If you have any questions Send an Email or check-out the FAQ page
